SPEAKER NOTES — Entropy-Guided Token Importance for PPO
========================================================

SLIDE 1: The Problem
--------------------
- Start by explaining that in RLHF with PPO, we optimize a policy over
  sequences of tokens, but the standard loss treats every token equally.
- Give the math example: in "Let me calculate 15 × 7 = 105", the token "105"
  is where the model actually needs to get it right. The "Let me" part is
  boilerplate — it doesn't matter for the reward.
- But PPO gives the same gradient weight to "Let" and "105". This is wasteful.
- TI-DPO showed that weighting tokens by importance improves DPO. Can we do
  the same for PPO?

SLIDE 2: Why This Is Hard
--------------------------
- Key point: DPO is offline (single pass), PPO is online (hundreds of steps).
- In DPO, you have paired sequences — you can compare chosen vs rejected
  token-by-token. That gives a natural importance signal.
- In PPO, you just have one sequence and a scalar reward. There's no
  contrastive signal to derive importance from.
- The critical issue: any non-uniform weighting introduces BIAS in the gradient
  estimator. In DPO (single pass) this doesn't matter. In PPO (hundreds of
  steps) this bias ACCUMULATES and eventually hurts.

SLIDE 3: TI-DPO Background
---------------------------
- Briefly cover what the TI-DPO paper does — they weight the DPO loss per-token
  using gradient attribution, attention scores, etc.
- Their methods work well for DPO (+2-5% on preference benchmarks).
- Our starting point was: let's port these to PPO.
- Mention that these methods have varying computational costs — gradient requires
  a backward pass, attention is free, etc.

SLIDE 4: Why Entropy, Not Advantage
-------------------------------------
- THIS IS A CRUCIAL SLIDE. Spend time here.
- In standard PPO for LLMs, the reward is a SINGLE SCALAR at the end of the
  sequence. GAE propagates this backward with discount factor 0.95.
- So advantage at token t = 0.95^(T-t) × reward. This is purely POSITIONAL.
  It doesn't depend on what the token IS, just where it IS.
- Using |advantage| as importance = "weight later tokens more". That's not
  a meaningful content signal.
- Entropy is different: it measures the model's UNCERTAINTY at each position.
  High entropy = multiple plausible continuations = the model hasn't decided
  what to do = this is where learning should focus.
- Entropy is free (computed from existing logits) and content-aware.

SLIDE 5: Entropy Intuition
---------------------------
- Walk through the concrete example token by token.
- "Let" has low entropy — there's really only one way to start.
- "=" has HIGH entropy — the model needs to output the answer and there are
  many possible numbers it could generate. This is the critical decision point.
- "105" also has high entropy — this is the actual answer.
- The key insight: entropy naturally identifies DECISION POINTS where the
  model's choice determines whether the answer is correct.
- These are exactly the tokens where PPO's gradient signal is most valuable.
- Ask the audience: if you could only update the model on ONE token, which
  would you pick? The "=" or the "Let"? Entropy tells you this automatically.

SLIDE 6: The Degradation
--------------------------
- Show that fixed entropy weighting works great initially (+10%) but then
  degrades and performs worse than baseline.
- The explanation: early on, high-entropy tokens are genuinely uncertain
  decision points. But as the model learns, the entropy landscape shifts.
- What was high-entropy before (genuine uncertainty) becomes high-entropy
  for different reasons (e.g., the model has learned one pattern and is now
  exploring alternatives).
- The biased gradient keeps pushing on these tokens even though they're no
  longer the right ones to focus on.

SLIDE 7: Bias-Variance Tradeoff
---------------------------------
- This is the mathematical formalization of what we observed.
- The key equation: Bias = Cov(w, f). If weights correlate with gradient
  magnitude (they do — that's the point!), then the estimator is biased.
- Early: variance is high (model is far from optimal, gradients are noisy).
  Importance weighting reduces variance → net benefit despite bias.
- Late: variance is low (model nearly converged). But bias has accumulated
  over hundreds of steps → net harm.
- This is why EVERY fixed-intensity method eventually degrades. It's not a
  bug in entropy specifically — it's a fundamental property.
- This also explains why TI-DPO works: it's offline (single pass), so bias
  doesn't have time to accumulate.

SLIDE 8: AITI
--------------
- The fix is elegantly simple: decay the intensity over training.
- ε starts at 1 (full entropy weighting) and decays to 0 (uniform PPO).
- Early: you get the variance reduction benefit of entropy weighting.
- Late: you get the unbiased gradients of standard PPO.
- Analogy to learning rate scheduling: LR controls step size, AITI controls
  gradient quality. Both should start high and decay.
- We tested linear decay and residual decay (ε → 0.3 instead of 0).

SLIDE 9: MOAI
--------------
- AITI requires choosing a decay schedule (how many steps? linear? quadratic?).
- MOAI removes this hyperparameter by computing the optimal ε per step.
- The MSE criterion balances bias² and variance. Closed-form solution.
- Monotone constraint: ε can only decrease, preventing oscillation.
- Key finding: MOAI converges to ε ≈ 0.3-0.5, NOT zero. This means the
  optimal strategy is permanent partial weighting.
- Interpretation: there's always some benefit to focusing on uncertain tokens,
  even late in training. You just shouldn't weight them as heavily.

SLIDE 10: Synthetic Results
----------------------------
- On synthetic rewards (GPT-2, sentiment task), both AITI and MOAI
  Pareto-dominate vanilla PPO.
- MOAI gets highest absolute reward (+2.4% over baseline).
- AITI gets best KL-efficiency (same reward with 2.5× less KL drift).
- Fixed entropy weighting is dominated — confirming the theory.
- This is the cleanest result because the synthetic setup has no confounders
  (no answer extraction, no chat template issues).

SLIDE 11: GSM8K Results
-------------------------
- Moving to real math: GSM8K grade-school problems.
- IMPORTANT: mention the implementation challenges we had to solve.
  The fp16 bugs were critical — without fixing them, training literally
  didn't work (KL=0, NaN values).
- The chat template fix was worth +12% accuracy — using the model's proper
  instruction format matters enormously.
- Results: all methods beat the base model by 8%. Entropy methods match
  the baseline. The task may be too easy — base model already at 55%.
- The story here is more about getting the pipeline right than about the
  method itself.

SLIDE 12: MATH-500 Results
----------------------------
- Harder task: competition-level math.
- PPO itself barely improves the 1.5B model on MATH (31.6% vs ~30% base).
- With such a small PPO improvement, there's not much for entropy weighting
  to amplify.
- Currently running with a 4B model and 500 episodes to get stronger results.
- Mention that proportionally, entropy methods capture a larger share of the
  PPO gain on MATH than on GSM8K.

SLIDE 13: Batch Size Finding
------------------------------
- THIS IS ONE OF THE STRONGEST FINDINGS.
- At batch size 1: entropy methods give +4-5% over baseline. This is a
  real, significant improvement.
- At batch size 8: the gap vanishes.
- Why? Token importance is variance reduction. With bs=1, gradient variance
  is extremely high (one sample!). Entropy weighting helps by focusing the
  gradient on the most informative tokens.
- With bs=8, the gradient is already stable (averaging over 8 samples), so
  there's less variance to reduce.
- Practical implication: if you're training with small batches (common in
  RLHF due to memory constraints), entropy weighting is free improvement.

SLIDE 14: Takeaways
---------------------
- Four main points:
  1. Use entropy, not advantage, for token importance in PPO
  2. Fixed entropy weighting degrades — decay the intensity
  3. AITI (scheduled) and MOAI (adaptive) both work
  4. Most valuable in high-variance settings (small batch, sparse reward)
- The core insight: token importance is a variance reduction technique.
  Like control variates or baselines in RL, it helps when variance is the
  bottleneck but doesn't help when bias is the bottleneck.

SLIDE 15: Future Work
-----------------------
- Scaling: we need larger models where PPO makes a bigger difference.
  The 1.5B model on MATH barely improves with PPO at all.
- Longer training: 500-1000+ episodes to see the full bias-variance dynamics.
- Harder tasks: code generation (where token importance is very natural —
  variable names vs logic), tool use, multi-step reasoning.
- Methodological: apply entropy weighting to the KL penalty too, not just
  the policy loss. Combine with GRPO (which is gaining popularity).
- Theoretical: can we derive the optimal decay schedule analytically,
  not just the MSE-optimal ε per step?
- End with the framing: this is a variance reduction technique. Simple,
  free, and complementary to everything else in the PPO pipeline.
